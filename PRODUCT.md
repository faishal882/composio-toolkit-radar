# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Composio's product and engineering reviewers, scanning many intern take-homes on a laptop and deciding within roughly two minutes whether the candidate can turn ambiguous integration research into a trustworthy operating system.

## Product Purpose

Toolkit Radar is a self-explanatory case study of 100 requested apps. It must reveal the build-vs-outreach portfolio, make every row auditable, demonstrate a runnable Composio-powered research agent, and show exactly how verification improved a weak first pass.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, candid, operator-minded. The page should feel like a sharp internal decision memo that happens to be unusually polished—not a candidate trying to impress with decoration.

## Anti-references

- A spreadsheet dump with no recommendation.
- A generic SaaS landing page full of floating cards and unearned claims.
- A dark terminal cosplay that makes 100-row research harder to scan.
- A glossy case study that hides misses, uncertainty, or gated evidence.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the portfolio decision, not the methodology.
2. Make every claim one click away from its evidence.
3. Show misses as product insight: identity locking and uncertainty are features.
4. Reward a two-minute skim, then support a twenty-minute audit.
5. Treat the agent and the human review loop as one system.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA or better, keyboard-operable filters, semantic tables, visible focus, non-color status labels, responsive layouts, and a reduced-motion path. Body text should meet at least 4.5:1 contrast and primary reading text should target 7:1.
